Benefits of pushups:



What are the advantages of performing daily pushups?


Pushups are an excellent way to make your upper body stronger. The triceps, chest muscles, and shoulders are all worked out. When through with proper form, they will also strengthen the lower back and core by engaging (pulling in) the abdominal muscles. Pushups are a simple and effective strength-building exercise. They may be completed from almost anywhere and do not require any special equipment. If you're looking for a consistent fitness program, doing a set of pushups every day can help. If you practice pushups on a daily basis, you'll probably see an increase in upper body strength. For the simplest results, still add variety to the kinds of pushups you are doing . you'll also follow a “pushup challenge“ where you gradually increase the amount of pushups hebdomadally . you can get up to 100 reps in two months of good workout.

Disadvantages of doing pushups daily


One disadvantage of doing anybody exercise a day is that your body will not be challenged after a short time . That increases your risk of plateauing. This happens because your muscles adapt and improve their function once they are stressed. So it’s important to still challenge your muscles to enhance your strength and fitness level. If you’re getting to do pushups every day , having the right form is additionally important. Doing pushups without proper form can cause an injury.If you don't do pushups correctly, for example, you'll have lower back or shoulder pain. If pushups are too difficult initially , modify the exercise. Do them on your knees or on a better surface. See a physiotherapist before doing pushups if they are too hard on your wrists or if you have had a previous wrist injury.

How to perform pushups?

To perform a standard pushup:
1-Start kneeling on an exercise mat or the ground and convey your feet together behind you.
2-Bend forward into a high plank, the highest pushup posture, with your palms flat on the mat, hands shoulder-width apart, and fingers pointing forward or slightly in.
3-Your shoulders and your hands should be in a straight line. You should keep your feet together behind you and your back should be flat.
4-Keep your abs pulled in.
5-Slowly lower your body toward the ground .
6-Maintain a rigid torso and keep your head aligned together with your spine. Don’t let your low back drop or your hips elevate.
7-Continue lowering yourself until you make contact with the ground with your chest or chin.
8-Your elbows may flare during the downward movement.
9-Press upward with your arms together.
10-Continue pressing until your arms are fully extended at your elbows and you’re back within the plank, at the highest of the pushup position.
11-Repeat the downward movement.

Start with 4 sets of 5 pushups, or as many as you can do with proper form, and add more while you're getting stronger.
